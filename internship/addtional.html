üèπ Sena Medal (Army)

The Sena Medal is awarded to soldiers of the Indian Army for acts of exceptional courage and devotion to duty. Introduced in 1960, it recognizes bravery not necessarily entailing direct contact with the enemy. It may be awarded for gallantry in operations such as counter-insurgency, mine clearance, disaster relief, or humanitarian missions.

Rifleman Aurangzeb of 44 Rashtriya Rifles was awarded the Sena Medal posthumously in 2018 for his brave decision-making during a terrorist ambush in the Pulwama sector. Despite being off duty, Aurangzeb responded to help a stranded patrol under fire. He engaged the terrorists, creating a diversion that enabled his unit to regroup and rescue civilians. His selfless action cost him his life‚Äîbut his courage saved many.

Naib Subedar Sanjay Kumar, PVC (Param Vir Chakra) also holds the Sena Medal for continued gallantry after Kargil. In 2004, while posted in J&K, he single-handedly charged a terrorist hideout under intense gunfire, killed three terrorists, and secured his unit‚Äôs flank, ensuring no civilian or soldier casualties. Regarded as a soldier‚Äôs soldier, he exemplifies fearless leadership beyond the battlefield.

Colonel Sofiya Qureshi, SM was awarded the Sena Medal (Gallantry) for her exemplary leadership and courage during military operations and humanitarian efforts. A Signals Corps officer, she led troops during counter-insurgency, flood relief in Northeast India, and served with distinction in the UN Peacekeeping Mission in Congo. She made history in 2016 as the first woman to command an Indian contingent at the multinational Exercise Force‚Äë18, showcasing her operational skill and trailblazing spirit. In May¬†2025, she became the public face of 'Operation Sindoor' by co-leading media briefings alongside senior officials‚Äîfurther cementing her status as a national role model.

‚öì Nao Sena Medal (Navy)

The Nao Sena Medal is awarded to naval personnel for acts of exceptional courage and devotion to duty at sea. These gallantry awards honour those who go beyond the call of duty‚Äîduring rescue missions, anti-piracy operations, and crisis response under fire.

Vice Admiral Satishkumar Namdeo Ghormade served the Indian Navy with distinction from 1984 to 2023. He held the post of Vice Chief of the Naval Staff and played a vital role in naval modernization. An expert in navigation, he commanded ships like INS Brahmaputra and INS Nireekshak. He was instrumental in the commissioning of INS Vikrant, India's first indigenous aircraft carrier. He actively promoted innovation and indigenization through initiatives like SPRINT and iDEX. For his exemplary service, he was awarded the PVSM, AVSM, and Nau Sena Medal.

Captain Naveen Thapa was awarded the Nao Sena Medal (Gallantry) in 2019 for a daring night rescue after a Coast Guard sailing vessel capsized off Mumbai.

Piloting a Sea King at night in near-zero visibility and raging seas, he used night-vision goggles and steadfast piloting to hover over survivors, winch them aboard one by one despite strong winds‚Äîall while ensuring his own crew's safety.

He brought six personnel back to life through his courage, skill, and unflappable calm‚Äîearning widespread recognition for excellence in aeronautical rescue.

Commodore Jyotin Raina earned the Nao Sena Medal (Gallantry) for his extraordinary efforts post the Pulwama terror attack in 2019.

As Fleet Operations Officer of the Western Fleet, he led rapid naval redeployments‚Äîdeterring threats from enemy submarines and aircraft, expertly rerouting ships, and ensuring readiness in record time. His strategic vision and operational boldness proved critical in maintaining maritime security during a tense national moment.

‚úàÔ∏è Vayu Sena Medal (Air Force)

The Vayu Sena Medal is awarded to Indian Air Force personnel for courage or devotion to duty during aerial operations. It is often conferred for feats in aerial refueled missions, combat sorties, rescue, operational testing, or demonstration flying.

Wing Commander Abhinandan Varthaman shot down a Pakistani F-16 on February 27, 2019, during a dogfight across the Line of Control. He was captured but returned home two days later to a heroic welcome. Awarded the Vayu Sena Medal for courage and valor, his moustache and steely smile became a national symbol of Indian airpower and dignity.

Group Captain Varun Singh received the Vayu Sena Medal for a heroic save of a Light Combat Aircraft Tejas during routine testing in 2020. A malfunction that could have led to a crash was expertly managed in real-time, saving aircraft and pilot. His skill reflects the precision, courage, and technical mastery of IAF aviators. Group Captain Varun Singh later served as a key member of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), contributing to indigenous defence capabilities. He was also posthumously awarded the Shaurya Chakra for his bravery during the tragic 2021 helicopter crash in Tamil Nadu.

Wing Commander Akshay Saxena , an Indian Air Force pilot, was awarded the Vayu Sena Medal (Gallantry) for his role in an anti-piracy operation in the Arabian Sea in 2024, specifically part of Operation Sankalp. He piloted a C-17 Globemaster during the operation, which involved airdropping two raiding boats and 18 MARCOs (Marine Commandos) near the Somali coast to rescue a merchant vessel and capture pirates.The mission resulted in the rescue of 17 hostages and the capture of 35 pirates.
